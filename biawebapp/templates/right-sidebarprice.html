Electricity Consumption : Prediction

The practice of estimating how much energy will be used at a certain future period is known as energy consumption prediction. The ability to plan and deploy resources effectively, which may decrease waste and expenses, makes this a crucial role for energy organizations.
For predicting energy usage, a number of techniques may be utilized, including LSTM, SARIMA, ARIMA, and XGBoost. The choice of approach relies on the particular needs of the application and each method has strengths and drawbacks of its own.

Recurrent neural networks (RNNs) of the LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) kind are particularly effective for time-series data, such as data on energy usage. The LSTM can manage non-linear interactions between variables and can capture long-term dependencies in the data.
Traditional statistical models that may be used for time-series forecasting include SARIMA (Seasonal Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average) and ARIMA (Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average). A variant of ARIMA called SARIMA was created particularly for time-series data that displays seasonal trends. In order to provide forecasts, both models analyze the data to find trends, cycles, and seasonal patterns.

The machine learning method XGBoost uses decision trees as its foundation. It is a well-liked technique for predicting energy consumption since it can deal with non-linear interactions and can recognize intricate patterns in the data. Overall, the choice of approach for predicting energy consumption depends on a number of variables, including the quantity and kind of data available, the needed level of forecast accuracy, and the available computational resources.
